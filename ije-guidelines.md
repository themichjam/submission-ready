## 1. JOURNAL OVERVIEW

**Full name:** International Journal of Epidemiology  
**Abbreviation:** IJE  
**Publisher:** Oxford University Press (OUP) on behalf of the International Epidemiological Association (IEA)  
**Submission system:** ScholarOne ManuscriptCentral — http://mc.manuscriptcentral.com/ije  
**Guidelines URL:** https://academic.oup.com/ije/pages/general_instructions

**Scope:** IJE is the official journal of the IEA, covering epidemiological advances across communicable and non-communicable disease, health services research, preventive medicine, and new epidemiological and statistical methods. Published six times yearly; online-only. All articles free to view 12 months after publication.

**Key editorial policies:**
- All submissions must be in English
- Exclusive licence to the IEA required on publication
- Tobacco-industry-funded studies will not be considered
- Papers using data, infrastructure, or personnel from any low- or middle-income country will not be published unless at least one author is a national of that country
- No pre-submission service available
- Preprints on bioRxiv, medRxiv, and other servers are acceptable prior to submission; if accepted, authors must update the preprint status with the published DOI

### 2a. Original Article

**Scope:** Research papers, methods papers, systematic reviews, and meta-analyses.  
**Word limit:** ≤3000 (main text only; excludes Abstract, Key Messages, Declarations, References, Tables, Figures, Supplementary)  
**Abstract:** 250 words; structured with four headings in this exact order:
1. **Background**
2. **Methods** — must include study period and sample size
3. **Results** — must include main results with estimates and confidence intervals
4. **Conclusions**

**Keywords:** 3–10 keywords, placed at the end of the abstract  
**Key Messages:** Exactly 3 bullet points, each a single complete sentence, placed after the keywords and before the Introduction. The 3 bullets answer the following questions [verify the exact current wording at https://academic.oup.com/ije/pages/general_instructions — standard IJE practice is: (1) what is already known on this topic, (2) what this study adds, (3) implications for policy, practice, or research].  
**Required sections:** Introduction, Methods, Results, Discussion (IMRaD or equivalent), followed by Declarations.  
**Reporting guidelines:** STROBE required for observational studies; RECORD additionally required for routinely collected data; PRISMA encouraged for systematic reviews; MOOSE encouraged for meta-analyses of observational studies.

### 2b. Cohort Profile

**Purpose:** To provide up-to-date information about large population-based cohorts with planned long-term data collection. Individual cohort studies do not belong in this series — submit those as Original Articles.  
**Word limit:** 2500–3000 (main text only; excludes Key Features, Declarations, References, Tables, Figures, Supplementary)  
**Abstract:** None — if prompted during online submission, select or type N/A  
**Key Messages:** None — if prompted, select or type N/A  

**Eligibility requirements:**
- Baseline data collection must be complete with results beyond descriptive statistics
- Study must have completed prospective follow-up, or have funding/clear plans to do so
- Preference given to cohorts with >5000 participants at baseline
- Preference given to cohorts with follow-up data collected specifically for the cohort (not only from routine sources)
- At least one author must be a researcher involved with the data resource and available as a contact person
- Profiles must add substantially to information already available on existing websites
- Cohort consortia profiles must focus on the added value of the consortium and include at least one publication based on collated data from the constituent cohorts

**Required sections (in order):**
1. Title — must begin "Cohort Profile:" followed by cohort name in full (acronym in parentheses)
2. Author List — affiliations for all authors; both current and work-time affiliations if different; corresponding author marked *; editorial office retains the right to limit the number of authors on the title page; writing groups may list group name but must upload full author list as a support document
3. Why was it set up?
4. Who is in the cohort?
5. What has been measured?
6. What has it found? Key findings and publications — indicative summary, not exhaustive; illustrate one or two findings with a table or figure where possible; describe findings (what was found), not just topics examined
7. What are the main strengths and weaknesses? — cover both explicitly
8. Can I get hold of the data? Where can I find out more? — web address required if data are open access; identify at least one author as the designated contact person with contact details

**Pocket Profile (for print, ~500 words):** Required alongside every full profile. ~500 words including sub-headings; one simplified flow diagram, figure, or table derived or reproduced from the full profile. Required sections: Cohort purpose; Cohort Basics (location, year(s) of baseline data collection, N at baseline, population composition, age range); Follow-up and attrition; What has been measured?; Key findings; Strengths and weaknesses; Data access.

**Publication:** Full profiles published online only; one-page Pocket Profile in print.

### 2c. Cohort Profile Update

**Purpose:** To provide IJE readers with up-to-date information on cohorts already published in the IJE Cohort Profile series where the research focus or the data collected have changed substantially.  
**Word limit:** 2000–2500 (main text only; excludes Key Features, Declarations, References, Tables, Figures, Supplementary)  
**Abstract:** None — if prompted, select or type N/A  
**Key Messages:** None — if prompted, select or type N/A  

**Eligibility requirements:** Either (a) analyses on the new research focus must be complete so that some results can be presented, OR (b) at least one round of data collection including the new measures must be complete so that descriptive statistics for the new data can be presented.

**Required sections (in order):**
1. Title — must begin "Cohort Profile Update:" followed by the cohort name exactly as it appeared in the original Cohort Profile
2. Author List — affiliations for all authors; writing groups may list group name but must upload full author list as a support document
3. The original cohort — brief description citing the original Cohort Profile publication
4. What is the reason for the new focus (or new data collection)? — why the focus changed; what the new research areas will be; why new data were collected
5. Who is in the cohort? — date of most recent data collection; current age of participants; who remains in the cohort and how they differ from participants at baseline
6. What has been measured? — description or table of new measures; brief details for any unusual measures; descriptions of sub-group measures if applicable; new data linkages
7. What has it found? Key findings and publications — findings related to the new research focus, or descriptive statistics and basic associations for the newly collected data
8. What are the main strengths and weaknesses? — cover both explicitly

**Pocket Profile (for print, ~500 words):** Required alongside every Update. Sections: The original cohort; The new focus; Who is left?; New Measures; Key findings; Strengths and weaknesses; Data access.

**Publication:** Online only; one-page Pocket Profile in print.

### 2d. Data Resource Profile (DRP)

**Purpose:** To make the research community aware of data resources that fall outside the rubric of a cohort study. Typical examples include the Human Mortality Database, the Indian National Family Health Survey, large randomised controlled trials, and clinical case series with long-term follow-up.  
**Word limit:** 2500–3000 (main text only; excludes Key Features, Declarations, References, Tables, Figures, Supplementary)  
**Abstract:** None — if prompted during online submission, select or type N/A  
**Key Features:** Up to 5 bullet points of complete sentences, ≤200 words in total; placed prominently at the start of the profile; summarises the most important characteristics of the data resource

**Eligibility requirements:**
- Data access must be open via a website, OR the author(s)/institution must have the right to grant at least collaborative access via a straightforward and transparent application process
- At least one wave of data must have been collected and made available

**Required sections (in order):**
1. Title — must begin "Data Resource Profile:" followed by the descriptive title
2. Author List — affiliations for all authors; corresponding author marked *; writing groups may list group name but must upload full author list as a support document
3. Key Features — up to 5 complete-sentence bullets, ≤200 words total
4. Data Resource Basics — area covered by the data resource; start and end dates of the period covered; number of units, groups, or individuals surveyed; dates and numbers for each repeat survey if available
5. Data Collected — dataset production including source data, sampling strategy, software/hardware if relevant; data type and survey type; ethical clearance if appropriate; funding sources; measures collected at each level (e.g. individual, household) indicating collection method, data type, and frequency; measures to ensure data quality; anonymisation techniques
6. Data Resource Use — one or two illustrative examples of how the data resource has been used or can be used; examples of publications
7. Data Resource Access — summary of opportunities for data access and collaboration; contact details or web links; whether data are in a public repository or available on request; funding and competing interests; standard acknowledgement paragraph required by the data holder, verbatim or indicated where it can be found
8. "Profile in a nutshell" box — placed at the end of the Data Resource Access section; ≤200 words; written as brief complete sentences covering: why set up and/or unique features; date established, area, number of units/groups/individuals covered; source data, sampling strategy, data type; main categories of data collected

**Pocket Profile (for print, ~500 words):** Required alongside every full DRP. Sections: Data Resource Basics; Data Collected; Data Resource Use; Reasons to be cautious; Collaboration and data access; Funding and competing interests.

**Publication:** Full DRPs published online only; one-page Pocket Profile in print.

### 2e. HDSS Profile

**Purpose:** To provide up-to-date details of Health and Demographic Surveillance Systems (HDSS) across the world, providing readers with sufficient information to understand scope, plan collaborations, and maximise use of existing resources.  
**Word limit:** 2500–3000 (should be succinct and indicative rather than exhaustive)  
**Summary:** 150–200 words; free form (not structured); must outline: location of the HDSS, why it was set up, participants, data collection methods, main categories of data, and data access. Included in the main text document.  
**Key Messages:** 3–4 short bullet points; must summarise the main or unique features of the HDSS that are NOT characteristic of any other INDEPTH HDSS site; may include scientific findings; must not repeat material already in the Summary.

**Eligibility requirements:**
- Must have collected at least one round of data
- Must have either completed prospective follow-up of some participants, or have funding and clear plans to do so
- Some data access must be available
- IJE will consider updates of existing HDSS profiles where research focus or data collected have changed significantly

**Required sections (in order):**
1. Title — should include the HDSS name
2. Author List
3. Summary (150–200 words, free form)
4. Key Messages box (3–4 bullets)
5. Background and rationale — why the HDSS was set up; original research questions; major changes or developments since inception; current questions addressed (if section is not relevant, it may be omitted)
6. Location and map — must include a map or series of maps; text must provide co-ordinates and describe the area (terrain, seasons, temperature range, rural/urban character, main employment, healthcare facilities); major data collection centres noted where relevant
7. Data collected — all individuals and groups on whom data are collected; number of data collection rounds; dates of each round at each level (individual, household, group); flow diagrams encouraged; if ongoing, indicate how often data will be updated
8. What has been measured?
9. What has it found? Key findings and publications — indicative summary; illustrate with a figure or table where possible
10. Specific existing and planned analyses — concrete plans for future analyses so external researchers can identify existing/planned work and avoid duplicating it; indicate if collaboration in planned analyses is welcome
11. Strengths and weaknesses — cover both explicitly

**Optional:** Authors may include photographs if they can grant OUP permission to publish them (this is a suggestion, not a requirement).

### 2f. Software Application Profile (SAP)

**Purpose:** To present practical and current software applications for use by the epidemiological research community, enabling readers to assess whether the application is suitable for their research goals.  
**Word limit:** 1500–2000 (main text only; aspects may exceed these guidelines if necessary, but explain in the cover letter)  
**Abstract:** 150 words; structured with four headings in this exact order:
1. **Motivation** — why the software was developed; what problem it solves
2. **Implementation** — programming language, platform, key technical details
3. **General features** — what the software does; key capabilities
4. **Availability** — where to access it; URL required

**Keywords:** 3–10 keywords, placed at the end of the abstract  
**Key Messages:** 3–5 succinct bullet points; placed after the abstract  
**Maximum display items:** 2 figures or tables combined  
**Title:** Must include the name of the software application and concisely describe its use  
**Style:** Avoid overly technical jargon in the abstract, introduction, example usage case, and discussion/conclusion

**Software eligibility requirements:**
- Must be a non-trivial application requiring limited effort to install
- Must be freely available to non-commercial users
- Must be made available for a minimum of 3 years following publication
- Web browser-based applications or services must not require user registration
- Must run under a variety of conditions; must be compatible with multiple operating systems or hardware architectures where applicable
- Accepted types: desktop applications, web browser-based applications or services, server or command-line applications, plugins

**Required sections (in order):**
1. Title (software name + description of use)
2. Author List
3. Abstract (150 words; Motivation / Implementation / General features / Availability [URL])
4. Keywords (3–10)
5. Key Messages (3–5 bullets)
6. Introduction — background and motivation; avoid excessive jargon
7. Example usage case — practical worked example illustrating real-world application
8. Discussion or Conclusion — limitations; future development plans
9. Declarations
10. References

### 2g. Education Corner

**Purpose:** To provide easily accessible, concise updates and reviews of epidemiological concepts and methods suitable for educational use. Articles should be suitable for use as course notes or background material in epidemiology, biostatistics, and related fields, and should introduce important methods without being overly technical.  
**Word limit:** NOT SPECIFIED in the main guidelines — verify at https://academic.oup.com/ije/pages/Education_Corner  
**Summary:** Unstructured; ≤250 words  
**Keywords:** 3–10 keywords, placed at the end of the Summary  
**Key Messages:** Exactly 3 bullet points, placed after the Summary and before the Introduction. The 3 bullets answer Education Corner-specific questions [the exact questions are listed in the full guidelines — verify at https://academic.oup.com/ije/pages/general_instructions; these differ from the Original Article Key Messages questions].  
**Published:** Free to View — anyone may read and use with attribution; indexed and counted as regular publications.

**Two sub-types:**
- **Educational notes:** Provide understandable, step-by-step presentations of how a method works, aimed at answering "Where can I find an understandable presentation of this topic?"; intended for undergraduate or postgraduate students; should include at least one multiple-choice question to test knowledge and reasoning
- **Methods comments and news:** Comment on established methods that are currently misused or misinterpreted, or present recent cutting-edge methods explaining their use, purpose, range, and limits of application

**Style requirements:**
- Tutorial style, step-by-step format strongly encouraged; if alternative style used, justify in the initial proposal or cover letter
- When discussing advanced or complex models, provide background and context (e.g. an article on accelerated failure time models should briefly review survival methods and survival functions)
- Methods must be presented within the context of related methods and empirical tools so readers can assess alternatives
- Further background and context may be provided in online supplementary material
- Must NOT present substantially new methods not yet applied in epidemiology (submit those as Original Articles)

**Required sections:** No rigid section structure mandated; must include Summary → Keywords → Key Messages → Introduction → body content; step-by-step tutorial format strongly encouraged.

**Submission process:** Authors must first submit an **initial one-page proposal** to the editors before submitting a full manuscript. Non-English speakers should engage a competent scientific or medical translator from the proposal stage onward.

### 2h. Photo Essay

**Purpose:** Visual essays on topics relating to health and well-being. Broad definition — essays examining political, social, and environmental determinants of health are welcome.  
**Word limit:** 200–500 words of text  
**Number of photographs:** 10–15  
**Resolution:** High resolution required  
**Reproduction:** Colour in the online version; black and white in print; reproduced free of charge  
**Permissions:** Authors/photographers must be able to provide OUP with permission to reproduce all images used. Authors/photographers retain the right to reproduce their own photographs.  
**Abstract:** None  
**Submission:** Via ScholarOne as for other article types

### 2i. Letters and Responses

**Purpose:** To comment on published articles and other material in the journal; encouraged by the editors.  
**Word limit:** NOT SPECIFIED — verify at https://academic.oup.com/ije/pages/general_instructions  
**Abstract:** None  
**Submission:** Via ScholarOne  

**Requirements for Letters:**
- Must be objective, constructive, and evidence-based
- Must provide new or useful information, an alternative viewpoint, or alternative experience
- Each specific point stated separately for ease of reading
- All comments substantiated; no general or biased criticisms
- Published at the Editor's discretion

**Requirements for Responses:**
- Lead author of a response is responsible for contacting all authors of the original paper to ascertain whether they wish to be included in the reply
- Submitted via ScholarOne

## 3. REQUIRED MANUSCRIPT STRUCTURE

### Universal requirements — all article types

**Title page** (included in main document) must include:
- Full title (short, specific, keyword-based descriptive phrase; NOT a complete sentence; NO subtitle; no excessive methodological detail unless methods are the primary focus)
- Short running head directly below the title on the title page
- All author names and affiliations; if current affiliation differs from affiliation at time of work, both must be given
- Word count
- Corresponding author: postal address + email address; one corresponding author only; marked with asterisk (*)
- Joint first/senior authors: marked with superscript symbol (e.g. †) and explanatory footnote (e.g. "†Joint first authors")

**File requirements:**
- Main document: editable Word file (NOT PDF), designated 'Main document'
- Exception: LaTeX manuscripts submitted as PDF initially; Word or LaTeX source files required at acceptance
- Double spaced; margins of 2.5 cm; all pages numbered
- Headings and table/figure titles in **sentence case** (first word and proper nouns only capitalised)
- **Footnotes NOT permitted in the main body** — footnotes permitted only in tables and figures
- Tables and figures may be included in the main document at submission; if accepted, must be provided as separate files

### Declarations — required order for all article types

All declarations appear at the end of the manuscript in this order:
1. Ethics approval (typically stated in Methods section where applicable)
2. Supplementary data (if applicable): "Supplementary data are available at IJE online"
3. **Funding** — mandatory; begins "This work was supported by…"; full official funding agency names; placed **before** Acknowledgements; if none: "Funding: None"
4. **Acknowledgements** — optional; minor contributors only; all named persons must have consented
5. **Author contributions** — mandatory; specific and detailed per author; for ≤25 authors: in the manuscript; for >25 authors: as supplementary file, with manuscript text reading "Author contributions are available as a Supplementary file at IJE online"
6. **Conflict of interest** — mandatory; placed **after** Funding and Acknowledgements; if none: "Conflict of Interest: None declared"
7. **Data availability** — mandatory for all papers submitted from July 2022; all code underlying the paper must be made available; data sharing strongly encouraged where ethically feasible
8. **AI tools disclosure** — required if AI tools used in data collection, analysis, or image/graphical element generation; disclose which tools and how in the **Methods** section AND in Declarations; AI tools cannot be listed as authors

## 5. REFERENCE STYLE

**Style name:** Vancouver (modified IJE style)  
**In-text citations:** Arabic numerals in square brackets [1], [2,3]; numbered in order of first appearance in text, tables, and figures  
**Reference list:** Numbered sequentially in order of appearance  
**Author rule:** Surnames and initials of ALL authors when ≤6; when ≥7, list first 3 then add "et al."  
**Journal abbreviations:** Index Medicus style  
**DOI:** Include where available; required for preprints  
**Footnotes in text:** NOT permitted; use references instead

### Worked examples in IJE Vancouver style

**1. Journal article — ≤6 authors, with DOI**
> Davey Smith G, Ebrahim S. 'Mendelian randomization': can genetic epidemiology contribute to understanding environmental determinants of disease? Int J Epidemiol. 2003;32:1–22. doi:10.1093/ije/dyg070

**2. Journal article — ≥7 authors (first 3 + et al.)**
> Lawlor DA, Harbord RM, Sterne JA, et al. Mendelian randomization: using genes as instruments for making causal inferences in epidemiology. Stat Med. 2008;27:1133–63.

**3. Book**
> Rothman KJ, Greenland S, Lash TL. Modern Epidemiology. 3rd ed. Philadelphia: Lippincott Williams & Wilkins; 2008.

**4. Book chapter**
> Clayton D, Hills M. Analysis of follow-up studies. In: Armitage P, Colton T, editors. Encyclopedia of Biostatistics. Chichester: Wiley; 1998:1414–26.

**5. Website / online source**
> World Health Organization. Global Health Observatory Data Repository. https://www.who.int/data/gho (20 January 2024, date last accessed).

**6. Report / grey literature**
> Office for National Statistics. National Life Tables, United Kingdom: 2019–2021. Newport: ONS; 2022.

**7. Preprint (DOI required)**
> Surname A, Surname B. Preprint title. medRxiv 2023. doi:10.1101/2023.01.01.123456

### Common errors to watch for
- For ≥7 authors: must use first 3 + "et al." — do not list all authors; do not use 6 + "et al."
- Using full journal names instead of Index Medicus abbreviations
- Omitting page ranges — first and last page required
- Numbering references alphabetically rather than in order of appearance
- Using (1) or superscript numbers instead of [1] in text
- Omitting DOI when available
- Using abbreviated funding agency names (full official names required)

## 6. FIGURES, TABLES & SUPPLEMENTARY MATERIAL

### Figures

**Accepted formats:** eps, tiff, png, or jpg — do NOT submit in PDF, docx, or pptx  
**Minimum resolution:** 300 dpi at a minimum width of 300 mm (3600 pixels wide)  
**Colour:** Photos reproduced free in colour online and black & white in print. Colour charges for other figures: NOT SPECIFIED — verify with journal  
**Numbering:** Consecutive Arabic numerals  
**Legends:** Required for every figure; self-contained — each figure must be interpretable without reference to the text; include meaningful title, legend, and adequate footnotes  
**Abbreviations:** All abbreviations in a figure must be defined in that figure's footnote — even if already defined in the main text  
**Labels:** Use legible axis labels — graphics software often produces figures with tiny labels that become illegible when resized for publication  
**Citation:** Each figure must be cited in the text  
**At submission:** May be included in the main document or provided separately; if accepted, each must be provided as a separate high-resolution file  

### Tables

**Format:** Editable Word or Excel format (NOT PDF)  
**Placement:** Separate from main text at acceptance; may be in main document at initial submission  
**Numbering:** Consecutive Arabic numerals  
**Titles:** Sentence case; meaningful and self-contained  
**Abbreviations:** All abbreviations defined in footnotes — even if already defined in the main text  
**Vertical rules:** Must NOT be used  
**Data layout:** Use separate rows for each line of data including sub-headings; do NOT use paragraph returns to separate data items within cells  
**Citation:** Each table must be cited in the text  

### Supplementary / Online-only Material

**Submission designation:** Each file uploaded as "Supplementary file (for online publication only)"  
**Reference in manuscript:** Add "Supplementary data are available at IJE online" under heading "Supplementary data" in the Declarations section  
**Author contributions >25 authors:** Provided as a supplementary file  
**Abbreviations:** Must be defined at first use in supplementary material — separately from definitions in the main text  
**Size/page limits:** NOT SPECIFIED — verify with journal  
**Labelling convention:** NOT SPECIFIED in main guidelines — verify; standard practice is Supplementary Table S1, Supplementary Figure S1, etc.

## 7. MANDATORY DECLARATIONS

### Mandatory submission forms (cannot complete submission without these)

**Authorship Form**
- Signed by the corresponding author on behalf of all authors
- Corresponding author ensures all listed authors meet ICMJE four-part criteria; no eligible contributor is omitted
- Upload via ScholarOne at initial submission

**Conflict of Interest (COI) Form**
- Signed by the corresponding author on behalf of all authors
- Complete only ONE of Section I or Section II on page 2
- Must be submitted even if there is no conflict of interest
- Upload via ScholarOne at initial submission

**Licence to Publish Form**
- Required at acceptance only (not at submission); completed online via link from OUP

### Funding (mandatory — placed BEFORE Acknowledgements)
- If none: "Funding: None"
- Opening sentence: "This work was supported by…"
- Full official funding agency name required — e.g. "the National Cancer Institute at the National Institutes of Health" or "National Institutes of Health" — NOT "NCI" or "NCI at NIH"
- Follow the Research Information Network (RIN)-approved list of agency names

### Acknowledgements (optional — placed AFTER Funding)
- For consultants and minor contributors only; not those who meet authorship criteria
- All named persons must have consented to being named

### Author contributions (mandatory)
- Specific and detailed per author — e.g. "AB designed the study. CD collected and cleaned the data. EF conducted the statistical analysis…"
- For ≤25 authors: included in the manuscript
- For >25 authors: provided as supplementary file; manuscript text reads "Author contributions are available as a Supplementary file at IJE online"
- No specific taxonomy (e.g. CRediT) mandated; contributions must be specific and detailed

### Conflict of interest (mandatory — placed AFTER Funding and Acknowledgements)
- Summarises all conflicts declared on the COI Form
- If none: "Conflict of Interest: None declared"

### Data availability (mandatory — all papers submitted from July 2022)
- All code underlying the paper must be made available (mandatory)
- All data on which conclusions rely should be made available where ethically feasible (strongly encouraged)
- Preferred approach: data in manuscript or supplementary material, or deposited in a public repository; code in supplementary material or public repository/code sharing platform
- See OUP Research Data Policy page for recommended repositories

### Ethics approval (required where applicable)
- Typically stated in the Methods section
- Must include the name of the approving body and approval reference number
- Participant consent must be addressed where relevant

### AI tools disclosure (mandatory if AI tools were used)
- Disclose which AI tools were used and how in the **Methods** section AND in the Declarations
- AI tools cannot be listed as authors
- Authors remain fully responsible for all AI-assisted content

## 8. REPORTING GUIDELINES

| Study Design | Guideline | Status |
|---|---|---|
| Observational studies (cohort, case–control, cross-sectional) | **STROBE** | **Required** |
| Studies using routinely collected health data | **RECORD** (STROBE extension) | **Required** (in addition to STROBE) |
| Systematic reviews / meta-analyses of RCTs and other evaluation studies | **PRISMA** | Encouraged |
| Meta-analyses of observational studies | **MOOSE** | Encouraged |

## 9. STATISTICAL REPORTING REQUIREMENTS

- **"Statistically significant" is banned** — do not use anywhere in the manuscript (Methods, Results, Discussion, or elsewhere)
- Do not write "findings at P<0.05 were considered significant" or any equivalent statement
- **Effect estimates** must be reported with **confidence intervals** throughout
- **Exact P values** must be reported — not P<0.05 or P=NS
- **P symbol:** Upper case, italicised — **P**
- **n symbol:** Lower case, italicised — **n**
- Statistical symbols are automatically typeset in italics — do not underline except to prevent ambiguity
- Symbols must not start a sentence
- For statistical manuscripts: clearly distinguish upper/lower case, ordinary vs bold-faced letters, Greek vs Roman letters, subscripts vs superscripts
- **Software disclosure:** NOT EXPLICITLY REQUIRED in guidelines — verify; best practice is to name statistical software and version

## 10. LANGUAGE, STYLE & FORMATTING

**English:** **UK English** required throughout — NOT US English  

**Abbreviations:**
- Keep to a minimum; avoid non-standard abbreviations
- Define at first use with abbreviation in parentheses in EACH of: (a) Abstract/Summary, (b) Key Messages/Key Features, (c) main text, (d) each table (in footnote), (e) each figure (in footnote), (f) supplementary material
- Do NOT use abbreviations in: titles, running heads, table/figure titles, headings, sub-headings
- Any abbreviation in a table or figure must be defined in a footnote to that item — even if already defined in the text

**Numbers:**
- One to nine: spell out in text
- Above nine: Arabic numerals
- Numbers followed by a unit: always numerals
- Must not start a sentence with a numeral
- Up to 9999: no separator; from 10 000 onwards: use a **space** as separator (e.g. 10 000, not 10,000)
- Per cent: **%** throughout (not "per cent")

**Units:**
- All measures in **SI units** with traditional units in parentheses in the text
- Exceptions: blood pressure in **mmHg**; haemoglobin in **g/dL**

**Age groupings:** Mid-decade groupings required (e.g. 35–44, or 35–39, 40–44); NOT full decades (e.g. do NOT use 30–39)

**Titles:** Short, specific, keyword-based descriptive phrase; NOT a complete sentence; NO subtitle; no excessive methodological detail unless methods are the primary focus

**Jargon:** Avoid throughout

**Footnotes in main body:** NOT permitted; use references instead

**Sentence case:** All headings and table/figure titles must be in sentence case
