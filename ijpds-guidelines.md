## 1. JOURNAL OVERVIEW

**Full name:** International Journal of Population Data Science  
**Abbreviation:** IJPDS  
**Publisher:** Swansea University (independent; not a commercial publisher)  
**Submission system:** IJPDS Open Journal Systems (OJS) platform — https://ijpds.org; requires registration with a **work email address**  
**Guidelines URL:** https://ijpds.org/author-guidelines  
**Open access:** Fully gold open access; CC BY licence for all articles; CC0 for source data  
**UKRI compliance:** IJPDS is UKRI Route 1 policy compliant (DOAJ listed); UKRI block grant funding may be applicable for eligible authors  
**Publication model:** Continuous publication throughout the year; special issues also published

**Scope:** IJPDS covers the science of population data at the **individual person level**. Submissions relating only to aggregated or statistical datasets are explicitly out of scope. The journal publishes across four categories: (1) data use for population impact; (2) bringing together and analysing data from multiple sources; (3) identifying population-level insights; and (4) developing safe, privacy-sensitive, and ethical infrastructure for research. The journal was established in partnership with the International Population Data Linkage Network (IPDLN) and has a regular section for IPDLN topics.

**Critical scope check:** Data must be at individual person level — submissions relating only to aggregated or statistical datasets are out of scope and will not be considered.

### 2a. Research Article

**Purpose:** Original quantitative or qualitative research within the scope of Population Data Science.  
**Word limit:** Verify at https://ijpds.org/author-guidelines (fee structure section); word count INCLUDES title page, main text, and reference list; EXCLUDES supplementary files.  
**Abstract:** ≤300 words; structured with five headings in this exact order:
1. **Introduction** — context and rationale
2. **Objectives** — specific aims or research questions
3. **Methods** — study design, data sources, analytical approach
4. **Results** — key findings, including relevant statistics
5. **Conclusions** — main conclusions and implications

**Highlights:** 3–5 bullet points, placed after the abstract; required for longer manuscripts; recommended for all Research Articles; should convey the focus and/or key findings of the article.

**Required format:** IMRaD — Introduction, Methods, Results, Discussion. This is mandatory. If IMRaD is genuinely inappropriate for the submission, contact the journal before submitting.

**Subheadings:** Use subheadings beneath each main IMRaD heading to organise content clearly. Each distinct method, result category, and so on should have its own subheading.

**Required section order:**
1. Title page (with full author details)
2. Abstract (structured; ≤300 words)
3. Highlights (3–5 bullets)
4. Introduction
5. Methods
6. Results
7. Discussion
8. Funding declaration
9. Ethics statement
10. Conflicts of interest statement
11. AI Disclosure Statement (if applicable)
12. Data Availability Statement (**placed just before the references**)
13. References

### 2b. Data Resource Profile

**Purpose:** To describe large population datasets of interest to Population Data Science researchers that are available for use, including survey (cohort and longitudinal), administrative, and digital footprint data collected at regional, national, or international level.  
**Word limit:** Verify at https://ijpds.org/author-guidelines (fee structure section); word count INCLUDES title page, main text, and reference list; EXCLUDES supplementary files.  
**Abstract:** ≤300 words; unstructured  
**Key Features:** Mandatory; maximum 6 bullet points, each 1–2 sentences; placed prominently near the top of the manuscript; summarises the most important characteristics of the resource  

**Required sections (in order):**
1. Title — must begin "Data Resource Profile:" followed by the descriptive title
2. Author Details — full addresses for all authors (name, organisation, department, city, postcode)
3. Key Features — maximum 6 bullets, each 1–2 sentences; a description of what makes the resource unique and the key characteristics needed to decide whether it is suitable for use
4. Abstract — ≤300 words; unstructured
5. Background — why and how the dataset was produced; context for its creation
6. Methods — how the data resource was created; data collection, linkage methodology, quality assurance processes
7. Results — detailed description of the dataset: its structure, characteristics (demographic, clinical, social), and data quality
8. Discussion — strengths and limitations of the data; any projects currently using the dataset; suggestions for how other researchers could use it
9. Data Access — where and how researchers can access the data; contact details or web links; whether data are in a public open access repository or available on request
10. Conclusions — summary of the main conclusions and possible implications of using the data resource
11. Funding declaration
12. Ethics statement
13. Conflicts of interest statement
14. AI Disclosure Statement (if applicable)
15. Data Availability Statement (**placed just before the references**)
16. References

## 3. REQUIRED MANUSCRIPT STRUCTURE

### Universal requirements — all article types

**Title page** must include:
- Full title
- Author Details: for each author — full name, organisation, department, city, and postcode; entered in the 'Affiliation' open text box in the submission system; multiple author entries separated by a **semicolon (;)**
- One named corresponding author
- Manuscripts cannot be published without complete address information for all listed authors

**File format and formatting:**
- Submitted in editable format: **Word (.doc/.docx), OpenOffice, or RTF**; contact the journal if none of these formats is possible
- **Single-spaced; 12-point font**
- **Page and line numbers** must be included (to assist peer review)
- **Left-aligned text with ragged right margin** — do NOT justify text
- **Italics** for emphasis (not underline, except for URLs/hyperlinks)
- **One space** after a full stop at the end of a sentence
- **TAB** for paragraph indentation and for separating columns in tables
- Same elements (headings, labels, etc.) keyed consistently throughout the manuscript

### Declarations — required order

The following declarations appear at the end of the manuscript in this order (for Research Articles and other relevant types):

1. **Funding** — sources of funding; if the funder played any additional role in the study (e.g. study design, data collection), note this; if authors are required by their funder to note their salary source, include this; if no funding: state this explicitly
2. **Ethics statement** — mandatory for all research articles; must include (a) the name of the ethics committee, (b) the ID number of the approval, and (c) a statement that participants gave informed consent; OR an explicit statement that ethical approval was not required and the reason why
3. **Conflicts of interest** — all authors must complete the ICMJE Form for Disclosure of Potential Conflicts of Interest; if none: "None declared" will appear in the published article
4. **AI Disclosure Statement** — if any AI tools were used; must be present in both the cover letter and in the manuscript (see Section 7)
5. **Data Availability Statement** — mandatory for all research articles; placed **just before the references** (not at the very end of the manuscript)
6. **References**

## 5. REFERENCE STYLE

**Style name:** NOT SPECIFIED BY NAME in the accessible author guidelines. Based on published IJPDS articles, the journal uses a style consistent with Vancouver/NLM conventions. Verify the exact style requirements at https://ijpds.org/author-guidelines before submission.

**Critical:** The reference list is included in the word count for all IJPDS article types. This is unusual and must be accounted for when calculating total word count.

**General rules observed in published IJPDS articles:**
- References cited in the text and listed in a numbered reference list
- Authors listed by surname then initials
- For papers with multiple authors, et al. used after a threshold number of authors
- DOIs included where available
- Access dates included for online resources

### Reference examples from published IJPDS articles

**1. Journal article (with DOI)**
> Beduschi A. Synthetic data protection: Towards a paradigm change in data regulation? Big Data & Society. 2024;11(1). doi:10.1177/20539517241231277

**2. Journal article (multiple authors)**
> Drechsler J, Haensch AC. 30 years of synthetic data. Statist Sci. 2024 May;39(2):221–42. doi:10.1214/24-STS927

**3. Book**
> Drechsler J, editor. Handbook of Sharing Confidential Data: Differential Privacy, Secure Multiparty Computation, and Synthetic Data. Chapman and Hall/CRC; 2024.

**4. Book chapter**
> Dibben C, Raab GM, Nowok B, Williamson L, Adair L. Synthpop: A tool to enable more flexible use of sensitive data within the Scottish Longitudinal Study. In: Drechsler J, editor. Handbook of Sharing Confidential Data. Chapman and Hall/CRC; 2024.

**5. Conference proceedings / online report**
> Arthur L, Costello J, Hardy J, et al. On the Challenges of Deploying Privacy-Preserving Synthetic Data in the Enterprise [Internet]. In: Proceedings of the 1st Workshop on Challenges in Deployable Generative AI; 2023 Jul [cited 2025 Apr 18]. Available from: doi:10.48550/arXiv.2307.04208

**6. Website / online resource**
> Statistics Canada. Canadian Community Health Survey Data Linked to the Discharge Abstract Database. 2022 [cited 2025 Dec]. Available from: https://www.statcan.gc.ca/en/microdata/data-centres/data/cencchs-dad

**7. Preprint**
> Jordon J, Szpruch L, Houssiau F, Bottarelli M, Cherubin G, Maple C, Cohen SN, Weller A. Synthetic Data–what, why and how? arXiv. 2022 May 6. Available from: https://arxiv.org/abs/2205.03257

**Common errors to watch for:**
- Forgetting that the reference list counts toward the total word limit
- URLs provided without access dates for online resources
- DOIs omitted when available

## 7. MANDATORY DECLARATIONS

All of the following are required for Research Articles and most other article types. Each must appear in the manuscript. The Data Availability Statement must be placed **just before the references**, not at the end after references.

### Funding (mandatory)
- Sources of funding for the study must be declared at the end of the submitted manuscript
- If the funder played any role in the study (e.g. study design, data collection), this must also be stated
- Authors must note salary sources if required by their funder
- If no funding was received: state this explicitly

### Ethics (mandatory for research articles)
All submitted research articles must include a statement that either:
- The study obtained ethical approval — must include: (a) **the name of the ethics committee**, (b) **the ID number of the approval**, and (c) **a statement confirming that participants gave informed consent** before participating
- OR the study did not require ethical approval — must **state this explicitly and give the reason**

### Conflicts of interest (mandatory)
- All authors must complete the **ICMJE Form for Disclosure of Potential Conflicts of Interest**
- Conflicts may be personal, commercial, political, academic, or financial
- If no conflicts: "None declared" will appear in the published article

### Data Availability Statement (mandatory for research articles — placed just before the references)
- **Required for all submitted research articles**
- Must include: where the data is located, what criteria are necessary to access the data, and a DOI or URL link
- Required content varies by scenario:
  - **Openly available in a public repository:** repository name + DOI/URL + reference number
  - **Derived from a public domain resource:** name of resource + URL
  - **Available within the article and supplementary files:** state this clearly
  - **Provided by a particular organisation:** name of data provider
  - **Shared data published with the article:** written acknowledgement of consent to publish and openly share + funder/grant details if applicable

### AI Disclosure Statement (mandatory if AI tools were used)
AI tool use must be disclosed at **two separate locations**:
1. In the **cover letter** to the Editor
2. In an **AI Disclosure Statement** in the manuscript

The disclosure must cover any use of AI-assisted technologies (e.g. ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, Copilot, DALL·E, or similar tools) for: text generation, language editing, data analysis, image creation, or code development. This includes language polishing, summarising literature, improving readability, and drafting any parts of the manuscript.

AI tools cannot be listed as authors. All AI-generated content must be critically reviewed, verified, and appropriately edited by a human contributor. Authors remain fully responsible for the accuracy and integrity of all content, including AI-assisted portions.

### Author details (mandatory at submission)
Complete addresses for ALL listed authors must be provided in the 'Affiliation' field during submission: author's name, organisation, department, city, and postcode. Manuscripts cannot be published without this information. Multiple author entries are separated by semicolons (;) in the submission system.

## 8. REPORTING GUIDELINES

| Study Design | Guideline | Status |
|---|---|---|
| Studies using observational routinely-collected data | **RECORD** | **Required** — guidelines state authors should follow the RECORD statement |
| Randomised controlled trials | **CONSORT** | **Required** — guidelines state RCTs should be reported following the CONSORT statement; a CONSORT flow diagram illustrating participant flow through the trial is required |

| Systematic reviews / meta-analyses | **PRISMA** | NOT EXPLICITLY STATED in accessible guidelines — verify; standard practice for the field |
| Diagnostic accuracy | **STARD** | NOT SPECIFIED — verify |

| Qualitative research | NOT SPECIFIED — verify | |

**Clinical trial registration:** Clinical trials must conform to ICMJE recommendations. The registration number must be included in the manuscript. Secondary analyses of previously registered trials should include the original trial's registration number rather than being registered as a new trial.

## 9. STATISTICAL REPORTING REQUIREMENTS

- Report effect estimates with confidence intervals throughout
- Results section of the structured abstract must include "relevant statistics"
- Reporting of exact P values is standard best practice; IJPDS's specific policy on "statistical significance" language is NOT EXPLICITLY STATED in the accessible guidelines — verify, but note that many population data science and epidemiology journals discourage this terminology
- **Software disclosure:** NOT EXPLICITLY STATED as mandatory in accessible guidelines — verify; best practice is to name the statistical software and version used
- Code used in the analysis must be made available (see Data Availability Statement)

## 10. LANGUAGE, STYLE & FORMATTING

**Language:** All manuscripts must be written in **English**. No UK vs US English preference is specified — be consistent throughout.

**For non-native English speakers:** The guidelines recommend that text be revised using a professional manuscript editing company before submission (examples given: Cambridge Language Consultant, Write Scientific Right, Editage, Cambridge Proofreading LTD, Quality Proofreading by PSUK, Scribendi Inc.). Use of such services does not guarantee acceptance or preferential consideration.

**Formatting rules:**
- Single-spaced, 12-point font
- Page and line numbers included
- Left-aligned with ragged right margin — do NOT justify
- Italics for emphasis (not underline, except for URLs)
- One space after a full stop
- TAB for paragraph indentation and table column separation
- All elements keyed consistently throughout

**Subheadings:** Strongly encouraged throughout manuscripts. Use subheadings beneath each main heading to organise content. Each method, result category, etc. should have its own subheading.

**Population data science scope check:** Data must be at individual person level. Aggregated or statistical datasets only are explicitly out of scope.

**Work email required:** The corresponding author must use a **work email address** when registering on the submission system.

**Cover letter:** Required with every submission; must include:
- How the APC will be paid (self-funding or sponsored; if sponsored, sponsor details including sponsor name, billing address, VAT number)
- Disclosure of conflicts of interest
- Disclosure of AI tool use (if applicable)
- List of potential reviewers (optional but helpful; up to 3, no conflicts of interest, not from your immediate network or institution)

## 13. DRAFT AUDIT INSTRUCTIONS

When the user provides a manuscript draft (pasted text, uploaded file, or described content), follow these steps in order.

### Step 1 — Scope and summary check
In 3–5 sentences, describe:
- Whether the data used is at the **individual person level** — flag immediately if the submission appears to use only aggregated data (this is explicitly out of scope for IJPDS)
- Which of the four IJPDS Population Data Science categories the submission addresses
- The study design and data sources (e.g. linked administrative records, longitudinal cohort, routine health data, digital footprint data)
- The main research question or objective
- The approximate scope (N, time period, outcomes)

### Step 2 — Identify the submission type
Based on the draft:
- Which IJPDS article type best fits (Research Article, Data Resource Profile, Short Report, Viewpoint, Protocol, Letter, etc.)
- Note that the specific word limit for this type must be verified at the IJPDS fee structure page, as it was not accessible during skill construction; however, confirm that the word count method for IJPDS is total typeset content (title page + text + references), not main text only
- Which reporting guideline applies — RECORD is required for routinely collected observational data; CONSORT plus flow diagram for RCTs
- Whether a trial or study registration number is required
- State any uncertainty if the article type is ambiguous, and ask the user to confirm before proceeding

### Step 3 — Run the compliance audit
Work through every checklist item relevant to the identified article type:
- Mark ✅ **PASS** if the requirement is clearly met
- Mark ❌ **FAIL** if clearly not met — state exactly what is wrong and what is needed
- Mark ⚠️ **UNCLEAR** if compliance cannot be determined from the draft alone — state what information is missing

IJPDS-specific issues requiring particular attention:
1. **Scope:** data must be at individual person level — aggregated-only is explicitly out of scope
2. **Word count includes the reference list** — this is unusual; authors frequently undercount
3. **Data Availability Statement placement** — must appear just before the references, not after them
4. **Abstract headings for Research Articles** — must be Introduction / Objectives / Methods / Results / Conclusions; "Background" is wrong
5. **AI disclosure** — must be in both the cover letter AND as a separate AI Disclosure Statement in the manuscript
6. **Ethics statement** — must include committee name AND approval ID number AND consent statement (or explicit reason why approval was not needed)
7. **IMRaD** — mandatory for Research Articles; contact journal before submitting if genuinely inappropriate
8. **Data Resource Profile** — Key Features (≤6 bullets, 1–2 sentences each) is mandatory; Data Access section must include specific access instructions
9. **Text formatting** — single-spaced, 12pt, left-aligned, ragged right margin; not justified; TAB for indentation
10. **Highlights** — required for longer manuscripts (3–5 bullets after abstract, before Introduction)
11. **Author addresses** — must include organisation, department, city, and postcode for all authors; entries separated by semicolons
12. **Cover letter** — must state APC payment method; must disclose conflicts and AI use
13. **Work email** — required for corresponding author registration
